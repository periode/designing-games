systems

how do we make a game?

we design a system for interaction

meaning inside the game vs. outside the game?

Design is the process by which a designer creates context to be encountered by a participant from which meaning emerges.

Systems are groups of objects with attributes and internal relations that exist in an environment.

Interactivity is a cyclical process in which each of the participants subsequently speak, listen and think. The quality of the interaction depends on the quality of each of those parts.

we need to figure out:

  • what should the player(s) accomplish? (goals)
  • how should they accomplish it? (rules, mechanics)
  • why should they accomplish it? (story, theme)

what is the ultimate purpose? how do we qualify success?

can be main, secondary, multiple

can be explicit, implicit, emergent

overview

types of games

different configurations of rules, mechanics, input mechanisms and goals make for different types of games.

board games

a social event, reflect real-life, whether personal or political

hidden information through randomness (re: religion) or strategy (re: politics)

elegance means complex behaviour emerging from simple rules

sports

central role of skill, limited by the body

institutional events, based on the value of excellence (arete) and participation

adventure

spatial exploration, collecting objects, problem solving, storytelling

narratives as structures

games as logic

action games

more personal games, based on the development of computers (autonomous enemies)

positional, reflex based

a combination of emergence and progression

first person

often conflated with shooting games, male teenage aesthetic

switch to first-person exploration, focus on the environment and the sight (as a sense) rather than the sight (as weapon component)

role playing

projecting and transferring personnalities

leveling up and character development

combined with action or strategy

strategy

luck / action / strategy

strategy is: knowledge, information, calculation, predicting and guessing, certainty and uncertainty

importance of terrain, unit differentiation, resource management

strategy vs. tactics

rock - paper - scissors

simulation

making the best from the dynamic world around you

reaches into realism

tweaking the complex rules and influencing autonomous agents

casual

simple base mechanic

repetitive loop, small difficulty tweaks

very broad audience

economic model: free-to-play / pay-to-win

indie

experimental, small projects

a lot of personality

play with the standards and expectations of traditional video games practices

giving

getting

  2. do not interrupt

  5. respond and exchange
