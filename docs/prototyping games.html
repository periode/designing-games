systems

how do we make a game?

we design a system for interaction

  • arranging information/form, so that something makes sense meaning-making
  • problem-solving, functional aspect
  • purpose (function), budget (constraint)

rules can limit interaction - we can add context, incentive, objects (symbols, icons)

Design is the process by which a designer creates context to be encountered by a participant from which meaning emerges.

Systems are groups of objects with attributes and internal relations that exist in an environment.

Interactivity is a cyclical process in which each of the participants subsequently speak, listen and think. The quality of the interaction depends on the quality of each of those parts.

we need to figure out:

  • what should the player(s) accomplish? (goals)
  • how should they accomplish it? (rules, mechanics)
  • why should they accomplish it? (story, theme)

goals

what is the ultimate purpose? how do we qualify success?

can be main, secondary, multiple, external, internal

  • Reach the end of a series of actions, reach a particular state (no more cards, full resources, etc.)
  • Perform a series of actions within some constraints
  • Perform a series of actions better than another (group of) player(s)

rules

what are the creative constraints of the game?

can be explicit, implicit, emergent

if ... then ...

  • rules constrain the actions of players
  • rules decide on the evolution of the environment

There are hard rules, and soft rules. Hard rules are the ones that are enforced automatically by the computer (e.g. not being able to fly in Mario Kart), while the soft rules are the ones that need to be deliberately triggered by the player (driving into a banana) or broken by the player (finding hacks to jump across roads and platforms)

mechanics

what actions are at my disposal when it comes to achieving the goal within the rules?

what is the action players do most often?

players can ...

Actions can include movement (continuous decision, as in sports games), picks (momentary decisions, as in shooting games), moves (asynchronous decisions, as in chess).

storytelling

how do I make sense of what I do?

what can i interpret, and how? what is the symbolic part?

  • heart = life
  • key = open/access
  • coins/stars = exchange-value / bait / accumulation / achievement / perfection
  • doors = passageway
  • castles = place you want to get into
  • chests = valuable
  • impact marks = physical collision
  • shiny stuff = power-up

This is related to how elements are represented in the game (art direction), how events unfold in the game (on-rails vs. open-world, foreground vs. background), what kind of background/introduction is given to the player.
