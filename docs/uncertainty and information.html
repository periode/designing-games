randomness

randomness and information

what is the meaning of a dice roll?

  • a moment of tension, leading to happiness or sadness
  • luck, not having control but having to deal with consequences
  • consequences we don't control
  • relinquishing control to a 3rd-party object
  • the results makes us ask why?

a probability event (micro) gets transformed into a meaningful event through interpretation (macro)

meaning increases by the building of context (short/mid/long-term consequences, past knowledge of players and characters)

  • full randomness games (yahtzee)
  • partial randomness (backgammon, risk)
  • controlled randomness (unknown change within a known structure, dungeons and dragons -> fairness)
  • apparent uncertainty (civilization, fire emblem)
  • no randomness (but still uncertainty!), kids games, tag, monkey island, crosswords, sudoku, wordle

the feeling of uncertainty is more important than randomness itself

what are some aspects of uncertainty in your game? do they depend on mathematical randomness?

claude shannon, a mathematical theory of communication

sender -> channel (+/- noise) -> receiver

roman jakobson's functions of language

  • content of the message (poetic)
  • context of the message (situational)
  • tone of the message (emotive), reception of the message (conative)
  • establishment and maintaining of the message (practical)
  • playing with the message (metalingual), breaking the fourth wall
